About

Bridging engineering disciplines to create intelligent sensing systems.

I am a multidisciplinary researcher and engineer whose work bridges electrical and computer engineering, microelectromechanical systems (MEMS), and intelligent sensing technologies. Currently based in Buffalo, New York, I serve at M&T Bank, where I apply my technical expertise and analytical acumen to complex systems engineering and technology-driven innovation.

My overarching research endeavor is to develop multifunctional, miniaturized sensor platforms by integrating MEMS devices, flexible substrates, and embedded intelligence. My goal is to enhance real-time monitoring in healthcare and environmental applications, driving the next generation of adaptive, data-aware sensing systems that merge material science with artificial intelligence.

I have published influential works in journals such as Electronics Letters and Clinical Radiology, advancing the understanding of flex-circuit resonant chips, thermoresponsive capacitors, and wireless temperature tracking devices.

As a recipient of the Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council of Canada (NSERC) grant for Smart Material Transducers and Applications to In Vivo Microsystems, I have demonstrated leadership in the design of intelligent microsystems with biomedical and environmental impact.
